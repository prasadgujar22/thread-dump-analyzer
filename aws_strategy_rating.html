Critical Assessment: 100% AWS Strategy

Regulated Bank Infrastructure Rating
Dimension Rating /10 Critical Justification
Strategic Vision 3 "100% AWS" is vendor dogma, not strategy. Concedes architectural control to a single commercial entity. No systemically important bank operates this way.
Regulatory Feasibility 2 RBI, ECB, Fed classify single-vendor concentration as systemic risk. Data localization and jurisdiction conflicts make full compliance nearly impossible.
Operational Resilience 2 Multi-region within AWS is redundancy inside one control plane. When IAM, Route 53, or global console fails, every region fails simultaneously. No hedge.
Core Banking Suitability 1 Core systems require sub-millisecond latency and ACID consistency. Cloud adds cost, complexity, and failure modes with zero benefit for batch ledger processing.
Cost Efficiency 2 Predictable 24/7 workloads cost 20–40% more in cloud. Data egress penalizes integration. If DR on-prem is maintained (regulatory necessity), the bank pays twice.
Vendor Lock-in 1 DynamoDB, Lambda, Aurora, ECS Fargate create architectural entanglement. Re-platforming requires 5–7 years and complete rewrite. The bank cannot leave.
Innovation Velocity 5 Legitimate strength — AI/ML and digital product acceleration. But diluted by 2,000 annual AWS feature releases forcing continuous, expensive refactoring.
Talent & Skills 4 Junior AWS talent is abundant. Senior architects who understand banking regulation and core systems are rare, expensive, and easily poached.
Risk-Adjusted Return 2 Asymmetric downside. Best case: faster product launches. Worst case: total operational paralysis, regulatory sanction, or franchise-threatening costs.
Long-Term Sustainability 2 AWS controls pricing unilaterally. Geopolitical risk, service deprecation, and sovereignty mandates threaten the model. The bank becomes an AWS reseller.
Overall Critical Rating
2 / 10
